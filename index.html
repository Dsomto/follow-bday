here, then —

i on becoming +

You are still becoming. Don't rush the version of you God is still writing. The girl you'll be in five years is built in private — in mornings nobody sees, in books nobody knows you read, in prayers nobody hears.

Build her quietly. Let people meet her by surprise.

ii on love & marriage +

Don't pick a man who makes you small to feel big. Pick the one who would walk into a room and look for you. The one who treats your faith like a partner, not a competitor.

Wait well. Waiting is not wasted time — it is preparation. Use it. Heal in it. Become the woman the right man will struggle to deserve.

And listen — God is not slow. He is on time. He has never been late, even when it felt like it. Trust the season.

iii on beauty +

You are very, very beautiful. But your face is not your gift — it is your frame. The gift is what's inside it: your kindness, your faith, your laugh, the way you make people feel held.

Take care of your body anyway. Drink water. Sleep early when you can. Cream your skin. Eat real food. Take pictures. Wear the dress. Don't save your best self for "one day."

iv on this season — build +

Set goals you can write down. Then go beyond them. Don't aim at people's expectations — aim at God's. People will be surprised. He won't.

Pick one skill this year and get scary good at it. Pick one fear and walk into it. Pick one habit you've been hiding from and break it.

This season is your foundation. Pour it deep, even when nothing seems to be standing on it yet. Buildings come.

v on medicine — your white coat +

You picked one of the heaviest, holiest professions a person can pick. People will hand you their bodies, their parents, their children — at the worst moments of their lives — and trust you to do the right thing with them. That is not small. That is priesthood with gloves on.

The road is long. It is meant to be. The exams will try to break you. They will not. You were not made fragile.

Read until your eyes hurt. Then read a little more. Then sleep. Then come back. Discipline beats brilliance on a long enough timeline — and you have both.

And on the days you doubt the call: remember that you've already practiced. Every soul you've followed up on was a patient too — of a different kind. You've been training for this longer than you think.

vi on data analysis — your second wing +

Adding data to medicine is one of the smartest moves a young doctor can make this decade. Hospitals run on numbers nobody is reading well — you'll be the one who reads them well. That's leverage.

Start small and stay consistent. Excel first — really first. Then SQL. Then Python (pandas, numpy). Then a real project on real medical data — public datasets are free and full of stories waiting for someone like you.

Pick one course and finish it before you start another. Build, don't just learn. Show your work — even if it's only on your laptop. Done is a muscle.

You don't have to choose between healing and analyzing. The future of medicine is the doctor who can do both. Be that doctor.

vii on people you love +

Call your mother today. Call your father. Call the friend who used to text every day and now doesn't. People drift not because of malice but because of silence. Be the one who breaks the silence.

Tell people you love that you love them, while they can still hear you. Out loud. With their full name.

viii on money (since you didn't pick it) +

Make it. Don't worship it. Money is a poor master and a useful servant — keep it on that side of the line.

Save before you spend. Give before you save. Tithe before you give. In that order, somehow it always multiplies.

And if anyone ever offers you advice or money — take the advice. You already know.

ix on follow-up — the work you do +

What you do for the new converts is not "small." Heaven keeps a different kind of ledger than the one we keep down here. The names you remember, the texts you send, the people you check on — those things echo.

Don't get tired. And when you do get tired — rest, then come back. The harvest is real, and you are good at this.

x since I feel like you should know — +

Set goals. Real ones. Write them on paper. Don't just say "I want to be a doctor" — write what you'll do this term, this month, this week. Then go and actually do it.

Be strategic about everything. Don't pour effort where there's no return. Pour it where it compounds. School, books, skills, relationships, faith. In that order, somehow it always works.

And read crazy. Like, a lot. Books, papers, articles, anything. The doctor that wins is the one who reads when nobody is making her. You're already that kind. Now push it more.

Stay sharp. Stay growing. I'm watching. (in a good way.)
